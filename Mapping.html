Pitfalls of Data Vis. Mapping

1. Not using the appropriate map: As cairo mentions in his text, it is hard to project a sphere onto a flat space. One problem that cartographers/ data visualists run into is that they do not choose the proper map for the data that they are trying to choose. In the book, Cairo points to the Mercator map that that was developed for sea routes and maritime navigation. Because of this, Mercator does not accurately represent the true size of landmasses and would probably better be used for maps portraying sea travel or other things where land is not the main focus.
